djtl
What ideas or goals, if any, do you have with regards to the premiere tier?
HG | andrew 🏆 4/11/2023 4:15 AM
Last season's Premier had many of the perennial problems that Premier has experienced in prior seasons. However, there were some significant differences from Season 9. Here are some stats (correct me if any mistakes) that show the major differences from S9 to S10: Teams: 8 -> 6 Forfeits: 6 -> 0 Temporary contracts signed: 45 (1.05 / game) -> 47 (1.38 / game) Overall, I think most players would agree that a decrease in forfeit wins is a positive, whereas the decrease in team count and increase in subs per game are both negatives. My hope for the upcoming season would be that we can continue to minimize forfeits and decrease the number of subs needed. Increasing team count would also be great, although I think that is highly dependent on signup numbers and format changes will not greatly affect our ability to have more teams. I think all of these changes were caused in some part by the introduction of the pre-draft activity check. The check weeded out inactive players, and using the more accurate player count, we decided that Premier would only be able to support 6 teams instead of 8. Since more FAs were active, this made it easier for teams to find subs instead of forfeiting, resulting in both less forfeited matches and more subs used per match on average. In terms of improvements we can make, there are a number of ideas that have been brought up. As many people have mentioned before, we should do a poll near the beginning of next season to determine what schedule format changes might be best for Premier players.
  • We can consider changing the default days to different days that don't coincide as much with ESEA.
  • Reducing Premier to 1 match day per week is an option, but I would be hesitant to do this since I think this would be disliked by the players that only play CSC and not ESEA (poll results would prove or disprove this theory).
  • Another potential option is to loosen up the rescheduling rules for Premier, similar to what happened in Season 7. If the reschedule window is longer, it would likely be easier for teams to find a date and time that works for more of their players and reduce the number of subs needed.
  • Not a scheduling change, but we should implement a mid-season activity check to remove inactive FAs. This would lead to active PFAs being converted to FA, allowing teams more options for roster changes if their rostered changes are frequently unavailable (hopefully reducing subbing). Ultimately though, my philosophy with change is that we should be careful to not change Premier too much from the other tiers, and I would want overwhelming support from the Premier players before making significant changes from the typical format. Hopefully my wall of text was readable, if you or anyone else has questions on any of these specific ideas (or different ones), feel free to ask and I can expand more.

HG | andrew 🏆 4/12/2023 7:24 PM
I think a good example to answer your question would be some of the decisions we made regarding the schedule for this past season. We put out a poll to all players during preseason, gathering opinions on some scheduling topics (Valentine's day bye, play-in matches, etc.). We used the results of this poll to help make decisions on the schedule. For example, with over half of respondents saying they would not be able to play on Valentine's day, we decided that a bye was necessary for that day. In this case, the admin team agreed unanimously. On the contrary, deciding how many team would be in play-ins for Challenger was not a unanimous decision. In Challenger, the top 6 teams directly qualified to playoffs. The decision we had to make was whether play-ins would be between the 7th-10th place teams or the 7th-12th place teams. This had no other effects on the schedule and was mainly a matter of philosophy on how many teams should make playoffs. Some of us believed that having 12/14 teams in play-ins/playoffs was too many, other believed that it was better to include more teams in the postseason when possible. In the end, the vote was split 3-2 in favor of the 7th-12th format. Personally, I voted for the 7th-12th format, since I believed it was better to give more teams a chance to qualify into playoffs. I hope my answer gives you some perspective to the way that the admins discuss and make decisions.
