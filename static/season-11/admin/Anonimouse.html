PFA | Crimson King 4/12/2023 3:15 PM
I noticed that the CSC does raise money from the community to pay for each season. That is essentially how non-profits work, you raise money through donations and it's primary role is to provide a service or benefit to the community with no intention of earning a profit. CSC made it clear that they want to remain a not-for-profit community so becoming a non-profit is the best solution for the CSC if they ever wanted to become a legal entity. This is another reason I want to host charity streams. Hosting charity streams and funneling donations into known gaming related non-profits like Ablegamers and Extra Life would give the CSC some experience with working with non-profits before considering becoming one itself. I do not expect CSC to become a non-profit as admins may view it as too much of a risk. I only advocate for CSC to become a legal entity because of the funds raised to pay for seasons. According to the IRS, if someone (an individual or organization) were to donate more than $250, those records must be kept, among other things. Mainly, I just want the CSC to protect itself.
