For me, I want to go deep into the staff side of CSC, making sure that we're actively pursuing improvement and change when necessary. Be it a public or private matter, seeing important committees that are integral to our general function as a community stagnate is a bad sign. That being said, I think that for the most part those committees have been doing a great job the past season. Alongside this, having something along the lines of a mid-season check for the staff positions themselves could help with the current issues. Making sure that we keep active members involved may very well improve relations easily, as seeing active change or improvements to the committee and things that surround it can make the public more confident in their actions going forward. From personal experience, I've found that at times there's an issue with the "active" members of a committee becoming very lax in their position, be it that they're not supportive of change or aren't adding anything to the committee themselves, which is a quick way to stagnate and kill a portion of our community. Keeping active staff members would boost moral internally as well, as seeing new people who want to work and make change will most definitely get the ball rolling for many of those committees. What ideas or goals, if any, do you have with regards to the premiere tier?
ATL | omA üèÜ 4/11/2023 3:11 PM
There have been plenty of ideas and solutions brought up across the entirety of CSC, so I think that there are a few things to acknowledge before talking about how I personally would handle the issues. First off, it's been said many times that we are not intending to become a direct competitor to ESEA. I agree with this wholeheartedly, as I think trying to compete with a behemoth such as ESEA would be nearly impossible for us. Second, I think making major changes to the other tiers to accomodate Premier is a horrible pit to fall into. Potentially ruining the experience of other tiers to fix 1 tier is never worth it, and from players I've spoken to myself, they would hate to be forced into Premier by something like a tier-line shift. With that out of the way, I think making Premier MD a different day of the week wouldn't be a bad start/idea. It not only caters to those who are interested in doing both, but it allows for a highlight of Premier in terms of media. Having Premier MD be on something like Friday could open up the availability for lots of casters and observers in Media who can't participate otherwise, simply because they're actively playing on normal MDs. It gets us more consistent streams, and being able to show off the highest skill level we have to offer could bring in more players to our community. Another solution or improvement to Premier's situation would be an activity check for (P)FAs. For teams in Premier that are looking to make changes to their team or mix things up, being able to see or know who is actively interested in playing could drastically improve the overall experience and games in Premier. It would also hopefully decrease FFWs/FFLs that happen simply due to lack of players to sub in and out. Overall, I'd like it to be an open discussion with both the Admin team, GMs, and Premier players, as I think having a positive response from the majority is necessary when making a potentially large overhaul to any tier.
