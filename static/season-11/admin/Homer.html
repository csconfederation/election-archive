What ideas or goals, if any, do you have with regards to the premiere tier?
Homer 🏆🏆 4/10/2023 11:39 PM
I think the two largest reasons behind the problems in premier are scheduling conflicts with ESEA and the initial combine experience. For context, we had 61 projected premier players when we met to lock in the tiers for the season, and we ended up with 35 players drafted in premier for Season 10, which included a few players promoted by their GMs before the draft. I'm going to talk about the scheduling issue in this answer and then the combine issue in my response to RedondO. In my opinion, the schedule in premier has to be adjusted to allow players to work around their ESEA schedules to make CSC matches fit. The specific idea I've heard and am currently on board with came from yetiF, which is to replace the Tuesday-Thursday schedule with a once-a-week schedule. Not only would this remove one potential overlap with ESEA default days, but it would also allow for more flexibility when it comes to rescheduling CSC matches. The current rule allows matches to be rescheduled to before the original scheduled date and time or any time up to 11:59PM of the next match day except for that match day's normal match time. No matter what, I would like to get opinions from ESEA players on this before the season and get feedback after the season regardless of if an alternate schedule is implemented.

Homer 🏆🏆 4/10/2023 11:47 PM
This is a numbers solution specifically, but since I am implementing a potential solution I mentioned it in my application. Here's the rundown: A lot of new premier players tune out of the league right away, and I can't blame them based on the typical premier combine experience. In Season 10, premier had less than 20 combine games played, which is nowhere near any other tier. There were 20 players who played at least 20 combine games this season. The mentality of premier players is definitely a contributing factor to this, but I think being forced to sit in queue for 45 minutes because you missed joining the queue on your first night and then not even getting into a game at all because a few players left after the first one is an awful experience and would send people away. The biggest problem has been the inflexibility of numbers which doesn't allow for queues to be merged to get more games started. I've been working on the fix for that and just need to integrate it into the current system before it's ready to go for this next season.
