Why do you want to become an Admin? It is no secret that the past 2 seasons have been a little rougher than normal for the league. I believe admin inactivity and communication issues played a large part in the issues we've seen so far this year. I want to help guide the league back to a stable upwards trajectory in terms of player happiness, retention, growth, etc., and I am capable of putting in the hard work to achieve that.
10:28 PM
What prior experience do you have that you believe will help you as an Admin? I have been in and around the league for 6 seasons (S1-3 and S6-now), and played 5 seasons of RSC prior to that. In my time in the league I've been a GM for one season, AGM for 3, and captain for the other 2. I am also quite active in discussion of suggestions for league improvement. My experience with the league and its processes would make my transition into the Admin position much easier, and be useful knowledge to draw on when making decisions.
10:28 PM
What things do you hope to implement, reinforce, or remove as an Admin. First, I'd like to support the effort to document league processes. Over the seasons I believe a lot of knowledge has been lost during admin transitions, and a set of standard operating procedures would help ease new admins into the role and ensure consistency between seasons. For example, schedule templates for different sized tiers, which I believe Scraps created this past season, will make the creation of schedules much easier. I'm not too sure the extent to which our committees such as media, numbers or tech have their processes documented, but I would like to encourage committee heads and other knowledgeable committee members to do that. Secondly, I think the serious suggestions process could use a reform. It is disheartening as a player to make a suggestion that seems to have everyone's support, and then have it sit for weeks untouched because the admins forgot about it. I believe we should implement a process where after a certain amount of positive votes, the admin team is required to look into and produce an official response for the suggestion stating if it will be implemented, and if not, why. Besides those ideas, I hope to support other initiatives such as the reorganization of the discord. However, with all of these ideas, I cannot truly promise anything before the elections as I am not 100% familiar with everything that goes on behind the scenes of CSC.

UPS | Raasck
Do you have any ideas that can help with FA retention between seasons?
HG | andrew üèÜ 8/11/2022 2:11 PM
FA retention is always a tough issue to combat. We really have two main ways to work on this issue: Improving the FA experience with subbing in the actual league, and creating other events and experiences to keep FAs involved outside of subbing. With regards to outside events, we have the Trashmoe Cup in the offseason as well as the monthly wingman events that I think provide a good avenue for FAs to get involved in the league and keep them interested. I think I might suggest a version of the Trashmoe Cup that occurs midseason as an addition to the current events. This would be a good way to capitalize on the excitement of players that join midseason, and give them a taste of playing with the community if they haven't been able to sub yet as a PFA. As with anything, we might not have enough events bandwidth to make this happen, especially during the middle of a season, but I think it's worth looking into at the least. I have also seen some suggestions recently of a "Franchise Cup", this could also be something useful if we are able to incorporate FAs into it. For the actual FA experience in the league: This is the most important thing to focus on improving, although doing that is easier said than done I think. In my mind at least, public MMR goes a long way to helping players understand why they are cut, and helps FAs understand why they might be unable to sub for certain teams. Also, the rules preventing a GM from backing out on a sub is a good protection to have in place for FAs. I know subbing can be a less than fun experience for FAs, but I don't have many great ideas on how to improve it. I know the end of season feedback form asks FAs about their experience, so if that information is not being used, then we should make sure it is. Maybe there could be a use for a midseason feedback form to capture the opinions of unsatisfied players that may not be around by the time the end of season form is sent out.

@GAS | Stynky @PFA | Crispy @ATO | Assass1n Apologies for taking a while on this one, I wanted to make sure my answer was high quality. I'm going to try to give one answer that addresses all of your questions, as I think they are related. If I miss something or you need more information please let me know. The number one thing I want to do as admin is help create standard operating procedures for the admin team and encourage committees to do the same. For the admin team, this would be things like having a planned schedule for how the 3 seasons should fit into a year, so we never have any issues with not being able to end our last season on time. Or, another example, documenting how to process signups. For a committee, say media, this could be how a new observer can set up their system to observe CSC matches. Maybe these specific things have already been done, but the general idea remains. Having these procedures documented will make it easier for new admins or committee members to be brought up to speed. Additionally, it will help all of staff do their work quicker and with fewer mistakes. Lastly, distributing knowledge among committee members will reduce the amount that certain committees depend on one or two members to function. All in all, saving time on the day to day tasks will allow admins more time to create new things to better the league. To answer the question of what longstanding impact I would like to have, it would be that. I would like things to be set up so that any new admin can easily come in and start working, and not have to spend too much time on mundane tasks, or spend a lot of time learning how to do those tasks. Ultimately, more effective admins and committees will improve player experience in the league, which is the end goal that should be driving most if not all decisions.
