Admin Seasons 8-9 Multiple staff positions leading up to being elected Admin (former TM Head and former Mod Head are most notable) ---------- What things do you hope to implement, reinforce, or remove as an Admin? My past two seasons I have spent time *trying* to make things easier for current and future Admins. This has been primarily through documenting processes and making sure other Admins know how to do different aspects of the position. This is especially important in my eyes so a single Admin is not responsible for most of the knowledge of running the league. Historically, when one admin that knows a lot is no longer Admin, there is a period of ""what the fuck are we supposed to be doing and when"" which I dealt with a lot in Season 8. That being said, I have a few ways in which I have tried to make sure this information is shared. Some of these ways have been: - Sitting in VC and explaining/showing how to do certain things - Documenting different things - Master To Do List (posted in an admin channel - still adding to it but the most important information for the beginning of the new admin cycle is there) - ‚ÄúHow to‚Äù Document (shows/explains how to do different things in CORE, the RSC Bot, TicketTool, etc. ‚Äì Still a WIP and I will continue working on it even if I do not get reelected) - Transactions explanations in the GM Guide Document (project done by Andrew) + the transactions guide is planned to be its own thing next season (WIP) - Schedule ‚ÄúTemplates‚Äù for new seasons (these are hit or miss depending on how many teams are in a tier and how many match days there are, but they are a good base to reference) - Application/Voting Templates (just makes elections significantly easier to have a simple base to copy/edit from) There are still a number of things I would like to continue to work on from the list above as well as other private projects, but I will spare everyone some reading. I think involving myself as a player again will give me that perspective of the league that I have sort of lost touch with. Player perspective is important with making decisions/considerations, which is why I (and the team) has discussed mid-season feedback on top of the end of season feedback that is typically collected. A lot of what I *could* talk about for things that have been done the past two seasons were collaborative efforts, so I think it would be a bit unjust to take credit for things that I didn‚Äôt have a major impact on.

So personally, I have done a lot less this season than last season comparatively which has been a massive relief. This team has been an absolute blast to work with and I think everyone has stepped forward to get things done when necessary. I think the hard part with the admin position is that there are so many different ways people can approach this position. Some people might like interacting with the community more, some people might like documenting things, etc etc. So much actual labor goes into the offseason and then a lot of what happens during season is dealing with things that come up and making sure things are running smoothly and then just keeping up with random things that need to get done relating to timelines and whatnot. I tend to prefer to do jobs like documentation of different things, making sure player profiles with numbers are updated as necessary, checking in with some committees, schedule making, etc. I don't think that I do any of these jobs completely by myself, though. At the bare minimum I am checking in with other admins for feedback on something or asking if people are willing to do xyz. The job itself is really what you put into it. The only issue to consider if someone who wants to be admin but doesnt want to do a lot of leg work is I hope it is realized how much slack others have to pick up if one person isn't contributing. I always think the "why" someone wants to be admin is their most important answer during these times as it sort of tells me how involved they will actually be depending on how they answer the question. What I find valuable in that response might be different than what others find valuable, though.

In your opinion what is the worst thing about CSC?
scraps 12/12/2022 6:11 PM
I think the best and worst parts of CSC go hand in hand to be honest. I think a lot of us are super passionate about this league and want to see it prosper, but that also tends to be some of the worst situations to deal with as emotions are super high. I am definitely not perfect in some of those situations, either. I think when conversations are pretty contained, even if they are passion-driven conversations, they go pretty well even if some people don't agree. There are plenty of things that have occurred even throughout my time as admin and I just try to understand the POV in which it is coming from. I don't necessarily think any idea is bad, I just think some things need to be fleshed out a bit more and I guess hope that the general public understands that at times there are 60 things on our list and we cant prioritize everything. Often times when things have been called out for not having an immediate admin comment it has been when a handful of things are happening at once and we are just trying to deal with everything. I personally tend to have the problem of skipping through channels and reading things, but not necessarily engaging with every interaction because I am digesting all the information. There are even times when we decide as a team to reply to something and discuss it internally before giving a public statement. There are also situations that people are collectively discussing things and then ask why an admin hasnt said anything yet and it could be because we are just watching the conversation happen and trying to understand what people are thinking about it or we just havent had time to read the conversation yet because of work/school. I have tried the method of acknowledging things even if i dont have a response in that moment and sometimes that just results in people saying they dont want me to acknowledge it, they just want an answer
